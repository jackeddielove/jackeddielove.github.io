Hello!

I am a mathematician, artist, and educator in San Francisco, California.

I hold a PhD in mathematics from George Mason University and a Masters in Design from California College of the Arts.

I have held faculty positions at George Mason University, San Francisco State University, and City College of San Francisco.

I was a founding co-director at San Francisco Math Circle Summer Camp and Outreach Director at Mason Experimental Geometry Lab at George Mason University.

I was an exhibiting artist at the Joint Mathematics Meetings in 2014 and 2019.

I am currently a volunteer at Mount Tamalpais College at San Quentin Rehabilitation Center. I also create fun math presentations for K-12 classrooms.

Math outreach
Philosophy

I define math outreach as:

The endeavor to create positive mathematical experiences for others.

I don't think that everyone needs to love math. I don't even think that mathematics is all that useful to most of us.

I do think that math is misunderstood and underappreciated as a creative practice, and I do outreach to grow that understanding and appreciation in others.

I do this by offering fun, engaging presentations and activities for K-12 classrooms, teacher development days, and any other spaces of learning and curiosity.

All activities are designed to be "low-floor/high-ceiling", meaning they are both accessible and challenging to any level of student.

Contact me to book a date or find out more.
