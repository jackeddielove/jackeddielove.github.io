Educational Philosophy

When we look at something deeply, it cannot help but reveal itself to us. Acquiring an education is not about accumulating secondhand knowledge from expert sources; it is about seeing new ideas arise naturally within ourselves as we practice deep looking.

This is ultimately a personal experience, but a supportive community of fellow travelers is a key component. Part of deep looking is clear communication. We often do not understand our own ideas until we can communicate them to someone else, at which point the idea itself may shift and change and grow.

My job as a facilitator of this experience is to lay the foundation on which we—whether we are a class, a workshop, or a tutor-tutee dyad—build that community.
